This application I created exhibits an animated bubble chart that shows how total population, GDP per capita, and life expectancy have changed in different countries over the past two centuries and how they will change in the next two decades.

For each year, you can hover your mouse pointer over any bubble to reveal the name of the country and its data during that particular time. To select a specific year, click and/or drag the timeline slider. To only see countries from a specific continent, select from the dropdown list above.

The data was downloaded from gapminder.org/data/. The population data and GDP per capita data included projected year values in the future but the life expectancy data did not, so I used linear regression to predict life expectancy for the years 2020-40 for each country.

The size of the total population for each country is denoted in the area of its bubble. The center of each bubble is the exact life expectancy and GDP/capita value. The variable life expectacy is defined by the average number of years a newborn child would live if current mortality patterns were to stay the same. The variable GDP per capita is defined by gross domestic product per person adjusted for differences in purchasing power (in international dollars, fixed 2011 prices, purchasing power parity (PPP) rates based on 2011 International Comparison Program (ICP) benchmark estimates.)

North America and South America are combined into a single category. Australia and Oceania are added to the Asian countries. This visualization contains information on 184 of the 195 countries. There are 11 countries not displayed because of a lack of sufficient data. Those countries not displayed are -

  • Liechtenstein (Europe)
  • Monaco (Europe)
  • San Marino (Europe)
  • Vatican City (Europe)
  • Dominica (West Indies, North America)
